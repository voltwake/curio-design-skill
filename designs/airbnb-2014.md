# Airbnb 2014

> Warm coral hospitality meets rounded humanist type — the visual language that defined sharing-economy trust.

## Origin

In July 2014 Airbnb unveiled the Bélo, a deceptively simple symbol designed by DesignStudio London to represent "belonging." The rebrand was led by CEO Brian Chesky (a trained industrial designer from RISD) and cofounder Joe Gebbia alongside DesignStudio's Ben Wright and Paul Stafford. It arrived at the crest of sharing-economy optimism — Airbnb had just crossed 10 million guests — and the symbol was simultaneously praised for its idealism and roasted for its Rorschach-test suggestiveness.

The accompanying visual system paired the coral-red hue **#ff5a5f** (named "Rausch" after Airbnb's first street address) with a proprietary humanist sans-serif called Cereal, generous photo-led layouts, and soft rounded corners everywhere. Karri Saarinen codified it all into the Airbnb DLS (Design Language System), one of the earliest large-scale design-token systems in the industry. The warmth and friendliness of the 2014–2018 visual language became the template for an entire category of lifestyle and marketplace products.

## Overview

Composition cues:
- **Layout**: Flexible photo-led layouts; hero imagery with overlaid search; card grids for listings
- **Content width**: Container (max 1280px) with generous side padding
- **Framing**: Solid white or cream surfaces; cards with thin borders and very soft shadows
- **Grid intensity**: Soft — structure is present but photography and whitespace dominate

## Colors

The Airbnb palette starts and ends with Rausch coral **#ff5a5f** — warm, energetic, instantly recognisable, but never aggressive. It sits on daylit white or the faintest warm cream (#f7f7f7). Text is deep charcoal #484848, with mid-grey #767676 for secondary copy. Supporting hues — Babu green (#00a699), Beach yellow (#ffb400) — appear sparingly for semantic roles and never compete with coral for attention. The overall impression is a sun-warmed photograph: high-key, inviting, and human.

**Role usage**:
- Page background → `colors.background.page` (#ffffff)
- Card/surface background → `colors.background.surface` (#ffffff)
- Subtle background (search bars, input fills) → `colors.background.subtle` (#f7f7f7)
- Primary actions (CTA buttons, links) → `colors.primary.500` (#ff5a5f)
- Body text → `colors.text.primary` (#484848)
- Secondary text → `colors.text.secondary` (#767676)
- Success/trust signals → `colors.semantic.success` (#00a699)
- Warnings/highlights → `colors.semantic.warning` (#ffb400)

## Typography

Manrope stands in for Airbnb Cereal: both are rounded humanist sans-serifs with open apertures, generous x-heights, and a warmth that reads as friendly rather than clinical. Headings use semibold or bold weights and slightly tighter tracking to feel grounded; body text stays at regular weight with relaxed line-height (1.5–1.6) so long-form descriptions of listings read comfortably. The overall voice is conversational, approachable, and confident without shouting.

**Text styles**:
- `display-xl` — Manrope, 96px, weight 800, line-height 1.0, letter-spacing -0.04em
- `display-lg` — Manrope, 64px, weight 700, line-height 1.1, letter-spacing -0.02em
- `heading-1` — Manrope, 48px, weight 700, line-height 1.2, letter-spacing -0.02em
- `heading-2` — Manrope, 32px, weight 600, line-height 1.25, letter-spacing -0.02em
- `body-lg` — Manrope, 18px, weight 400, line-height 1.6, letter-spacing 0
- `body-md` — Manrope, 16px, weight 400, line-height 1.5, letter-spacing 0
- `caption` — Manrope, 12px, weight 500, line-height 1.5, letter-spacing 0.05em
- `mono-md` — JetBrains Mono, 14px, weight 400, line-height 1.5, letter-spacing 0

## Spacing & Layout

- Base unit: 8px rhythm
- Scale: 2 / 4 / 6 / 8 / 12 / 16 / 24 / 32 / 48 / 64 / 96 / 128 px
- Container max-width: 1280px, centred with auto margins
- Section vertical padding: 64–96px on desktop, 32–48px on mobile
- Card internal padding: 0 (photo bleeds to edge), 16–24px for text content below
- Grid gap: 16–24px between listing cards

## Elevation & Depth

Airbnb's surfaces are almost flat — depth is communicated through very subtle shadows that hint at paper stacking rather than dramatic floating. Cards sit just barely above the page (2–4px blur, 5–8% opacity). On hover, cards lift gently with increased shadow. There are no glass effects or heavy blurs; the materiality is clean printed paper in warm daylight.

- Default card shadow: `0 2px 4px rgba(72,72,72,0.06)`
- Hover shadow: `0 4px 8px rgba(72,72,72,0.10)` with 2px upward translate
- No backdrop blur or frosted glass
- Focus ring: `0 0 0 3px rgba(255,90,95,0.25)` — coral glow

## Shapes

- Card corners: 12px radius (photo and container both rounded)
- Button corners: 8px radius (standard) or 9999px (pill variant for search bar)
- Input corners: 8px radius
- Avatar/thumbnail: 50% (circular)
- Modal/dialog: 16px radius

## Motion

Airbnb's motion is restrained and purposeful — transitions exist to confirm user actions, not to entertain. Elements ease in and out smoothly without bouncing or overshooting. Card hover lifts are gentle (250ms). Page transitions use simple fades. The overall feeling is calm competence: things respond, they don't perform.

- Level: restrained
- Default transition: 250ms, `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)`
- Fast micro-interactions (button press): 120ms
- Slow reveals (modal open, photo carousel): 400ms
- Hover patterns: subtle lift (translateY -2px), opacity fade, coral tint on icons
- Reduced motion: always respected; transitions collapse to instant

## Iconography

Airbnb uses clean, linear icons with rounded terminals that echo the Bélo's curves and Cereal's friendly geometry. Strokes are consistent at 1.5px, and icons appear at 20–24px in navigation and 16px inline with text. The tone is informational and warm — icons guide rather than decorate.

- Treatment: linear with rounded joins and caps
- Set: Lucide (closest open-source match to Airbnb's custom set)
- Stroke: 1.5px consistent weight

## Do's & Don'ts

### ✓ Do
- Use Rausch coral #ff5a5f for all primary actions and key visual anchors
- Let photography lead — large hero images, listing cards with bleed photos
- Keep backgrounds white or warm cream; embrace generous whitespace
- Use rounded corners consistently (8–16px) for a soft, approachable feel
- Write in a warm, conversational tone that matches the visual friendliness

### ✗ Don't
- Apply aggressive dark mode that loses the daylit warmth
- Use brutalist hard edges or sharp geometric treatments
- Introduce loud secondary colors that compete with coral for attention
- Fall back on cold corporate blue palettes
- Use serif headings — Airbnb commits to humanist sans-serif throughout

## Applications

This system is ideal for marketplace and hospitality platforms, travel and lifestyle products, community-driven SaaS, and any interface where trust between strangers is the core design problem. The warm coral, generous photography, and friendly type create an immediate sense of approachability that works especially well for consumer products where the brand needs to feel personal rather than institutional.
